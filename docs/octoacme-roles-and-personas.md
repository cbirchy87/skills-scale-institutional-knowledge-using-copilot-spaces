# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## Risk Manager

### Role Summary
Risk Managers proactively identify, assess, and mitigate project risks. They maintain the risk register, coordinate mitigation strategies, and ensure risks are communicated to appropriate stakeholders.

### Responsibilities
- Identify and document potential project risks during all phases
- Assess risk impact and likelihood using standardized criteria
- Develop and track mitigation plans with assigned owners
- Facilitate regular risk reviews with the Project Manager
- Escalate high-priority risks to leadership and stakeholders
- Monitor risk trends and update the risk register

### Goals
- Minimize project disruptions through proactive risk management
- Ensure risks are visible and understood by all stakeholders
- Reduce the impact of materialized risks through prepared mitigation plans

### Typical Communication
- Weekly risk review sessions with Project Manager
- Risk register updates and reports to stakeholders
- Escalation notices for high-impact risks
- Risk assessment during planning and retrospectives

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Project Manager**: Partners closely on overall project health; shares risk register updates
- **Product Manager**: Discusses risks related to scope, priorities, and customer impact
- **Developers**: Collaborates on technical risk identification and feasibility of mitigations
- **Stakeholders**: Communicates high-level risk status and mitigation strategies

---

## Communications Lead

### Role Summary
Communications Leads design and execute communication strategies to keep stakeholders informed and aligned. They ensure consistent messaging, manage communication channels, and coordinate announcements across teams.

### Responsibilities
- Develop communication plans for projects and releases
- Coordinate stakeholder updates and status reports
- Manage communication channels and distribution lists
- Draft and review project announcements and documentation
- Ensure timely and accurate information flow across teams
- Track communication effectiveness and adjust strategies

### Goals
- Maintain stakeholder awareness and alignment throughout the project
- Prevent miscommunication and information gaps
- Build trust through transparent and consistent communication

### Typical Communication
- Communication plans and schedules
- Stakeholder updates and newsletters
- Release announcements and change notifications
- Meeting facilitation for cross-team alignment

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Project Manager**: Coordinates on project status, timelines, and escalations
- **Product Manager**: Aligns on product messaging and customer communications
- **Risk Manager**: Receives risk information for appropriate stakeholder communication
- **Stakeholders**: Primary point of contact for project communication needs

---

## QA Specialist

### Role Summary
QA Specialists ensure quality standards are met throughout the development lifecycle. They design test strategies, execute validation activities, and verify that deliverables meet acceptance criteria.

### Responsibilities
- Develop test plans and test cases based on acceptance criteria
- Execute manual and automated testing across different environments
- Document and track defects with clear reproduction steps
- Verify bug fixes and perform regression testing
- Collaborate with developers on testability and quality improvements
- Validate releases before deployment to production

### Goals
- Prevent defects from reaching production
- Ensure features meet acceptance criteria and quality standards
- Maintain comprehensive test coverage and documentation

### Typical Communication
- Test plans and test case documentation
- Defect reports and validation summaries
- Quality metrics and testing status updates
- Testing requirements during sprint planning

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Developers**: Collaborates on test scenarios, defect resolution, and testability
- **Project Manager**: Reports testing progress, blocks, and quality risks
- **Product Manager**: Validates that acceptance criteria are testable and met
- **Change Control Coordinator**: Ensures changes are properly tested before approval

---

## Change Control Coordinator

### Role Summary
Change Control Coordinators manage the change request process, ensuring all changes are evaluated, approved, and documented. They balance the need for agility with proper governance and risk management.

### Responsibilities
- Review and triage incoming change requests
- Assess change impact on scope, schedule, and resources
- Coordinate change approval workflow with appropriate stakeholders
- Document approved changes and update project plans accordingly
- Track change metrics and trends
- Ensure changes align with project goals and constraints

### Goals
- Maintain project stability through controlled change management
- Enable necessary changes while preventing scope creep
- Ensure all stakeholders understand the impact of approved changes

### Typical Communication
- Change request forms and impact assessments
- Change approval decisions and documentation
- Change log updates and metrics reports
- Change review meetings with stakeholders

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Project Manager**: Partners on change impact assessment and schedule adjustments
- **Product Manager**: Evaluates changes against product priorities and roadmap
- **Risk Manager**: Collaborates on change-related risks and mitigation strategies
- **QA Specialist**: Ensures changes are properly tested and validated
- **Stakeholders**: Communicates change impacts and obtains necessary approvals
